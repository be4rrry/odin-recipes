Mississippi Pot Roast Recipe

Description

Being from Mississippi, this Mississippi Pot Roast is close to my heart. Although I didn't start making until I was out of college, I had it a good amount growing up. The amount of grease, fat, oil, and soul that goes into this dish makes enough sense as to why it's named after Mississippi.

There are a lot of other things you can add to this recipe as well. Some people put it on a sandwich, which personally, I am not crazy about. I like it was some potatoes and carrots, but others like it with some greens or just a whole 'nother stick of butter on the side. Anyway, let's get to how to make this dish.

Ingredients

  • 3-5 lbs of chuck roast
  • 6-infinite pepperochinis - I like mine hot, but the original recipe I followed called for 6. Now that is some weak stuff, so at least add about 10 if you're really want some good stuff.
  • 1 packet of ranch mix
  • 1 packet of au jus gravy mix
  • 1 stick of butter
  • (Optional) Yukon Gold Potatoes/Baby Red Potatoes
  • (Optional) Baby Carrots

Steps

  1. Combine all in crock pot, and set on low for 8-10 hours.
  2. Enjoy ;)
